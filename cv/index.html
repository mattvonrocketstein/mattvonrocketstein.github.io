Detailed Work History

Pilgrim Consulting (2017)

Telecommuter

Data Ops Consultant / Python Engineer

The music industry is kind of famous for having a data problem. Due to factors like an aging technology stack and a lack of cooperation amongst distributors, it's often the case that labels have difficulty getting at the data they need to make business decisions. In response to this problem, a client of Pilgrim Consulting had created an in-house data pipeline which I initially inherited, then benchmarked, documented, tested, and improved.

The project I inherited began life essentially as proof of concept or skunk-works, and as such it was very rough in many places. I learned the large codebase and began to polish it, and along the way fixed several hygiene issues such as adding basic testing and a more formal approach to dealing with secrets. I created terraform code that allows for parametric dev/test/prod environments, and aids in explicitly specifying and tracking the various compute/storage resources the overall system requires. This data pipeline involves technology such as S3, Redshift, Luigi, and highly algorithmic work on entity resolution. Feel free to ask me anything, but please be aware I possibly cannot answer much about the specifics of this work due to a restrictive NDA.

Datapipe Inc (2016 - 2017)

Telecommuter

Platform engineer

At Datapipe I was a Platform engineer, working with about 50 other people to build a next-generation PaaS offering called Trebuchet. The platform aimed to provide a cloud-agnostic set of tools that enabled customers to "just do development", without the time-consuming and costly process of struggling to get up to speed on modern microservice architectures, and devops in general. This job involved a huge number of technologies, and integrated various services written in Java, node, golang, python, and elixir, and deploying them using tools like terraform, kubernetes, helm, docker, and docker-compose.

As a member of the CI/CD team, I found myself at the intersection of all this action and working to enable and facilitate it all. Major achievements during my tenure were a migration from the corporate data-center into AWS, a transition from github enterprise to gitlab, and a transition from vanilla Jenkins to a resilient, robust, and highly-availabile Jenkins with elastic slaves. Using twistlock and sonarqube integrations with Jenkins, we also implemented quality gates based on static analysis of code and security vulnerability analysis of containers.

Gannett Media (2015 - 2016)

Telecommuter

Devops and Automation Engineer

For most of this engagement, I acted in both a planning and execution role to migrate existing applications, infrastructure, and data from Rackspace to AWS. During this migration, I converted all ad-hoc infrastructure to more formal infrastructure-as-code and outfitted all services with monitoring (cabot) and metrics (grafana+collectd). I consider this a major achievement because it was instrumental in helping both developers and product owners sleep better, knowing that any problems in their applications would be discovered immediately and result in the appropriate people being notified. I also created and deploying a mechanism for central logging via the ELK stack, so that should problems occur the average time spent debugging production issues would be drastically reduced. Applications involved in the data center migration were written in several languages and frameworks (golang, node+meteor, python+flask, and php amongst others). The bottom line? Safer and smoother operations and infrastructure, which ultimately save more than $200k a year on hosting costs alone.

Naxos Inc (2015 - 2016)

Telecommuter

C2H Infrastructure Architect / Automation Engineer

As part of a try-out for a full-time architect job, I designed and implemented key parts of a cloud-based music services stack in AWS. The system provided one-time downloads with mailinglist integration, using cloudfront as a CDN, written in python using the Django CMS, designed to be deployed with elastic beanstalk. I delivered programmatic infrastructure (puppet + fabric) for reusable microservices that do caching, configuration, scheduling, task queue workers, etc.

I also defined from scratch continuous integration work-flows and procedures for a team of about 20 developers, focusing on enabling iterative improvements without disrupting current developer throughput. I setup Jenkins with build jobs to execute the new work-flows. I learned how to use a new APM utility (datadog), and implemented various new monitors and alerts with it. I assisted with proof-of-concept migrations of existing applications out of legacy data-centers and into AWS, and addressed problems with existing manual data export tasks by creating automation for a more parallel ETL pipeline. The old approach was a tangle of bash scripts, whereas my new approach used multiprocessing and Amazon SQS for message queueing. Finally, I gained experience working with Redis both as a developer and an administrator.

Cox Media Group (2011 - 2015)

On-site in Atlanta // Telecommuter

Software Developer // Senior software developer // Platform engineer

As a developer for the parent company of the Atlanta Journal constitution, I was responsible for day to day feature implementation on one of the largest multi-site Django installations in the world. Our Django installation had about hundreds of models, hundreds of views, heavy ajax, and complex template inheritance. I participated in code review, requirement generation, design discussions, debugged production problems, and assisted with hot-fixes. I worked on document classification using OpenCalais and nltk, created internal and customer-facing APIs for everything from our "breaking news" alerts to metadata search features used by mobile apps. I also created highly customized, responsive interfaces for the Django admin panel.

I was promoted to senior developer and moved to the new "platform team" as the department grew from 10 to 50+ developers, and we were tasked with ensuring that sites could scale smoothly (both in terms our application performance in terms of growing teams ability to collaborate and maintain velocity). As a member of the platform team I worked closely with all kind of infrastructure and pilot programs for test-driving new infrastructure, including multiple versions of Solr for site search (using Pysolr and Haystack), Celery and Rabbitmq for application tasks outside of application request/response cycle. I helped to identify architectural problems and our technical debt, suggesting or implementing solutions. I also championed our "eat your own dogfood" approach to internal APIs, which helped us to break down a monolithic design into distinct services that could be deployed separately. I helped to define release procedure standards including load tests, integration tests, and infrastructure tests.

One achievement I'm most proud of was a complete overhaul of our testing approach, because prior to my work there it was a major bottleneck in our CI and a huge source of pain for QA. Our unit-tests included many dozens of in-house libraries, third-party libraries and forks, and feedback on whether we'd be able to release took hours after merges. By adding more Jenkins slaves and running the tests in parallel I was able to reduce the runtime to only 15 minutes while collecting more coverage data (including coverage data from integration tests).
