Strange day in BKK

Date Wed 31 August 2011 Category reports. Tags unfinished asia

Yesterday, i was going to get my laundry done and some locals driving by stop and we have this conversation:

"where you go?" "i go to get my clothes washed." "no." "no?" "no. today is no wash, is holiday. you go to see governor" "the governor??" "yes." silence

eventually i just get crammed into this beat-up car, i have no idea what's going on but my laundry plan was ruined anyway. we go off to the borneo convention center where they are celebrating Hari Raya, the end of Ramadan. see also: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eid_ul-Fitr

i'm feeling very underdressed suddenly because there are lots of funny hats, and colorful, but very formal dress around. i get the only-white-guy-in-the-room-award and shake hands with the prime minister on television, at this point still very confused about what is even going on. i get introduced to his whole family, they insist i sit with them and ask me where i'm from and everything.

turns out the custom is that, the farther a guest has come, the greater honor he bestows upon the host. they are very serious here about striving for tolerance/diversity, and i think that i was basically a publicity stunt. see also: OneMalaysia

today is also malaysian independence day-- double the party!
