Gear Philosophy

Suitable for multi-sport usage

Waterproof, waterproof, waterproof

Whether you're in for an expedition or just a short three day trek, water is the natural enemy of all outdoor enthusiasts. I strongly suggest going completely waterproof from the beginning, and not trying to accomplish this after the fact with pack-covers or internal dry sacks/trashbags. Unfortunately this can easily break the bank, but you can't put a price on increasing your confidence in the face of adverse conditions.

Lightweight but sturdy

The lighter your stuff is, the more food and water you can carry and the longer you can stay out in the big bad world. Ultralight backpacking gear is always nice, but don't be fooled into thinking it's sturdy.

Backpacks

I prefer completely waterproof backpacks in the 35-45L range, which tend to be fairly expensive technical mountaineering packs, and are usually top loaders. These packs require no pack cover. If you only carry a small bivvy sack for sleeping, and want a pack you can keep outside the tent even in a storm.. look no further. These both work well for bike-packing.. just toss it over your back two panniers and don't worry about mud/water getting thrown up on it. These packs are fairly small, and I almost always end up with improvised pockets strapped to the exterior. This works well if all your gear is lightweight, but keep in mind the shoulder-straps may be uncomfortable if you've overpacked. These are not 70L packs for the Appalachin trail or something!

Sea-to-Summit Flow 35L. Waterproof top-loader. Shape is rectangular when fully packed. This pack can supposedly carry skis but I think it would put a lot of wear and tear on the bag, and probably only last a few seasons. Very comfortable if not overpacked. This pack is extremely waterproof, probably up to a brief submersion.
Ortlieb Elevation pro 32/42 L. Waterproof top-loader. Shape is cyllindrical when fully packed. This pack has strong daisy chains running through the outside, and is suitable for strapping on ski's or a survival rifle. This pack is missing external mesh pockets and without Ortlieb add-ons or carabiners it doesn't have a good spot for a water bottle. Difficult to purchase in the US. This pack is fairly waterproof, but it cinches shut without a rolling top. Not suitable for submersion, and must be angled correctly (not upside down or sideways with respect to falling rain).
